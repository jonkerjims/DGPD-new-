Overview
Dense granular protein is secreted by the specific secretory organelle—dense granules of the Apicomplexans. It is involved in many biological processes such as vacuolar membrane material transport, host organelle recruitment, tissue formation of tube network and host immune response regulation and played an important role in the proliferation and pathogenicity of parasite. By the development of modern technologies, Toxoplasma gondii dense granule protein in different aspects and their functions are measured and reported, this will be an important way to reveal the mechanism of infection and pathogenicity.
