Introduction
Dense granular protein is secreted by the specific secretory organelle—dense granules of the Apicomplexans, which forms a large phylum of parasitic protozoa. It mainly modifies the parasitophorous vacuole (PV) and PV membrane in order to the maintenance of intracellular parasitism in almost all nucleated host cells.To explore and identify more new dense granular proteins and analyze their functions will be an important way to reveal the infection and pathogenicity mechanism of Apicomplexans.
Nearly every vertebrate and invertebrates can be the host of at least one apicomplexan species , such as T.gondii infection is related to reproductive failure of sows caused by abortion, fetal mummification, stillbirth and neonatal death in animal husbandry.Toxoplasmosis is one of the most important diseases that causing abortion of different female animals and it has great influence on animal husbandry and often leads to low productivity and economic loss.
Over the past 20 years, our understanding of the mechanisms of apical pathogenicity and virulence at the level of molecular structure as seen at atomic resolution has expanded dramatically.Unfortunately, this vast amount of knowledge has so far only concerned plasmodium and Toxoplasmosis.
Web Browser Requirements
DGPD requires a modern web browser that supports JavaScript and cookies. To view complex details, you cannot block pop-up Windows. The following browsers have been thoroughly tested by the database monitoring system:
  • Mozilla Firefox，Version 7 or above
  • Internet Explorer，Version 11 or above
  • Chrome，Version 8 or above上
The latest versions of Firefox and Chrome are recommended for visualization.
Evidence System

Levels of evidence for entries in DGPD.

Level NO. Category Definition
Level 1 Approved Items associated with FDA-approved and international guidelines.
Level 2 Clinical Items associated with clinical trials such as Phase I, Phase II, Phase III, clinical study and so on.
Level 3 Preclinical Items associated with in preclinical experiments such as preclinical-cell culture, preclinical-PDX, preclinical-cell line xenograft and so on.
What can users do in DGPD
  • Users can use keywords, genes and cDNA data.
  • Users can easily apply the search function that supports fuzzy queries to obtain content of interest in the field of cancer research.
  • The database monitoring system provides direct visualization of protein domain locations.
  • Users can download standardized data from the database for free.
  • Users can extend the database by submitting new data to us.
Usage
In the database monitoring system, we try to make it powerful and easy to use. This usage is intended for online services
Capitalized headings correspond to column headings in a web page table:
  • Gene: Official genetic symbol
  • Disease: Term for disease in the ontology of disease
  • Disease ontology identifier
  • Types: missense, FS-indel, repetition, complex-indel (simultaneous insertion and deletion)

1. Browse

You can select one or more of the options listed in the browse area (Organism and Gene).




2. Search

You can enter a keyword to search in the database management system. The search field includes species name and gene name.




3. Download

We provide the option to download the full database. If you want to download, please select the menu "Download" and click the Download button.




4. Contact

If you have any suggestions or questions not listed above, please contact us for details.
